What is an E-Scooter?

An e-scooter is a two-wheeled vehicle powered by an electric motor, designed for personal transportation. It can be ridden standing up and is often foldable for easy stora.

How far can an E-Scooter travel on a single charge?

The range of an e-scooter typically varies from 15 to 40 miles, depending on the model, battery capacity, and riding conditions.

How long does it take to charge an E-Scooter?

Charging an e-scooter usually takes between 4 to 8 hours, depending on the battery size and charger type.

Are E-Scooters safe to ride?

Yes, e-scooters are safe when used responsibly. Wearing a helmet, following traffic laws, and being aware of your surroundings can significantly reduce risks.

Can I ride an E-Scooter in the rain?

Many e-scooters have some level of water resistance, but it’s generally best to avoid riding in heavy rain to prevent damage and ensure safety.

Do I need a license to ride an E-Scooter?

Regulations vary by location. Some areas require a driver's license, while others do not. Always check local laws before riding
